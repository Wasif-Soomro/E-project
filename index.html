How to play

Game Setup Shuffle the deck and deal 7 cards to each player. Place the remaining cards face down (Draw Pile). Flip the top card to start a Discard Pile (if it's an action card, follow the effect). Rules:

Stacking Penalties: Official rules don't allow stacking Draw Two or Draw Four cards (house rules may vary). If the Draw Pile is empty, reshuffle the Discard Pile. How to play

Game Starts with a Jump Ball: The referee throws the ball in the air, and players jump to gain possession. Teams Move the Ball by Dribbling & Passing: Players must dribble (bounce the ball) while moving. Scoring Points: 2 Points: Made basket from inside the three-point arc. 3 Points: Made basket from beyond the three-point line. 1 Point: Free throw after a foul. Offense and Defense: The offensive team tries to score. The defensive team blocks, steals, and defends the hoop. 24-Second Shot Clock: Teams must attempt a shot within 24 seconds. Possession Changes After a Basket: The opposing team gets the ball and starts from their half. Game Continues Until the Final Buzzer: The team with the most points wins.

Rules:

Dribbling Rule: Players must dribble while moving. No running with the ball. Double Dribble: If a player stops dribbling and starts again, it’s a violation. Traveling: Moving without dribbling is not allowed. 3-Second Rule: An offensive player cannot stay in the key (paint) for more than 3 seconds. Backcourt Violation: Once a team crosses half-court, they cannot return to their own half. Shot Clock Violation: If a team doesn’t attempt a shot within 24 seconds, the opponent gets possession. Fouls and Free Throws Personal Fouls: Physical contact (pushing, hitting, tripping) results in free throws. Team Fouls: If a team commits 5 fouls in a quarter, the opponent gets free throws. Technical Foul: Unsportsmanlike behavior (e.g., arguing with referees) leads to a free throw for the opposing team. Flagrant Foul: Violent contact results in 2 free throws and possession for the opponent.

How to play

The first player/team throws the pallino to begin the round. The same player/team throws the first bocce ball, trying to land close to the pallino. The opposing team then throws a bocce ball, trying to get closer than the first ball. The team furthest from the pallino keeps throwing until they are closest or run out of balls. Rules:

How to play

Players take turns rolling two dice and move their token forward the total number of spaces. If they land on an unowned property, they can buy it from the bank, but if it is owned by another player, they must pay rent to the owner. How to play

Each player begins by drawing 7 tiles from the tile bag. The first player starts the game by placing a word across the center star square. On each turn, a player must place a word connecting to existing words, calculate the word score, and draw new tiles to maintain 7 tiles on their rack. Words must be found in an official dictionary, formed horizontally or vertically, and connected to previous words on the board. Each letter has a point value (e.g., "A" = 1, "Z" = 10), and bonus squares can increase scores: Double Letter (DL) doubles a letter’s score, Triple Letter (TL) triples a letter’s score, Double Word (DW) doubles the word’s score, and Triple Word (TW) triples the word’s score. If a player believes a word is invalid, they can challenge it using the dictionary. The game ends when no tiles remain and a player uses all their tiles, with the player holding the highest score declared the winner.

Rules:

No Proper Nouns: Names, brands, or places are not allowed. Using All 7 Tiles: If a player uses all their tiles in one turn, they get a 50-point bonus. Swapping Tiles: A player can choose to skip their turn and exchange tiles. Passing: Players can pass their turn if they can’t play a word.

How to play

A coin toss determines the first thrower. Players take turns throwing underhand from behind the foul line, with each player throwing all four bags before the next player takes their turn. Scoring is based on where the bag lands: 1 point if it stays on the board, 3 points if it goes through the hole, and 0 points if it lands off the board or bounces onto it. Cancellation scoring applies, meaning points cancel each other out; for example, if Team A scores 6 points (two bags in the hole) and Team B scores 4 points (one bag in the hole, one on the board), the final round score is 2 points for Team A (6 - 4 = 2). The first team to reach exactly 21 points wins, though an optional rule reduces a team's score to 15 if they exceed 21 points.

Rules:

Players must throw from behind the foul line. A bag that touches the ground first before landing on the board does not count. Players cannot remove bags from the board until the round is over. If a bag is knocked into the hole by another bag, it still counts as 3 points. In doubles, teammates cannot give advice or coach during play.

Number of Players 2 players (1 vs. 1) Chess Variants: Some versions involve multiple players or teams, but standard chess is strictly a two-player game.

How to play

To play chess, each player begins with 16 pieces arranged on an 8x8 board, ensuring a light-colored square is in their bottom-right corner. White moves first, and players alternate turns, strategically moving their pieces to attack, defend, and control the board. The objective is to checkmate the opponent’s king by placing it in a position where it cannot escape capture. Each piece moves differently: the King moves one square in any direction but cannot move into check; the Queen moves any number of squares vertically, horizontally, or diagonally; the Rook moves in straight lines vertically or horizontally; the Bishop moves diagonally across the board; the Knight moves in an L-shape and can jump over other pieces; and the Pawn moves forward one square (or two on its first move) but captures diagonally. Pawns that reach the opponent’s back rank can be promoted to any piece except a King.

Rules:

Chess follows specific rules to maintain fair play. Players must move only their own pieces and cannot skip a turn. A player is in "check" if their King is attacked and must make a move to escape; if no legal moves exist to remove the check, it is "checkmate," and the game ends. A special move called "castling" allows the King and a Rook to move simultaneously under specific conditions for added safety. "En passant" is a unique Pawn capture rule, and "stalemate" occurs when a player has no legal moves but is not in check, resulting in a draw. The game can also end in a draw if insufficient material remains to checkmate, by threefold repetition of the same position, or if 50 moves pass without a Pawn move or capture. Understanding these rules ensures a strategic and fair game.

How to play

Players are divided into two teams and positioned on opposite sides of the court, starting behind a designated line. Six dodgeballs are lined up in the center, and at the beginning ("Opening Rush"), players sprint to grab them. The objective is to eliminate opponents by throwing balls at them while avoiding being hit. Players can dodge incoming throws or catch a ball to eliminate the thrower and bring back a teammate. Rules:

Out of Bounds Rules Players must stay within their half of the court. If a player steps out of bounds, they are eliminated. Dead Ball Rule If a ball hits the floor, wall, or another surface before hitting a player, it does not count.

How to play

Teams are divided evenly, with equal players on each side. The rope is placed at the center mark. Each team lines up on their side, holding the rope firmly. The referee signals the start (whistle or "Ready, Set, Pull!"). Gameplay Players pull the rope toward their side using strength and teamwork. The goal is to pull the opposing team across the boundary line. The game continues until one team is pulled across the line or someone lets go of the rope.

Rules:

All players must hold the rope; no tying or looping around the body. No sitting or lying down while pulling. Hands must stay in their original position (no re-gripping). The team that pulls the center mark past their boundary line wins. The referee ensures fair play and stops the match if necessary. Fouls & Disqualifications Letting go of the rope intentionally results in a loss. Using unfair grips (tying the rope around hands or body) is not allowed. Dangerous pulling techniques (sudden jerks, twisting) can lead to disqualification.

How to play

Each player starts with 501 points. Players take turns throwing 3 darts per round. Subtract the score of the three darts from the total. The goal is to reach exactly 0 points. How to play

Game Setup Choose a Seeker – One player is selected to count while others hide. Set a Hiding Area – Define clear boundaries (e.g., playground, house, backyard). Designate a Safe Zone – A "home base" where hiders can run to avoid being tagged. Gameplay The seeker closes their eyes and counts (e.g., from 10 to 50) while others hide. Rules:

The seeker must count aloud with their eyes closed. Hiders cannot change hiding spots after the seeker starts searching. The game continues until all hiders are found or a time limit is reached. No leaving the play area (players must hide within set boundaries). If a hider reaches the safe zone before being tagged, they are safe.

How to play

Game Setup Two teams line up on opposite sides of the net. A coin toss decides which team serves first. Each team rotates players clockwise when they gain the serve. Basic Gameplay The server stands behind the baseline and serves the ball over the net. The receiving team must pass, set, and attack within 3 touches. The rally continues until: The ball lands on the ground (point to the other team). A team commits a foul or error. The team that wins a rally scores a point (Rally Scoring System). The first team to reach 25 points (win by 2) wins the set. If the game goes to a deciding set (5th set), it is played up to 15 points.

Rules:

In volleyball, each team is allowed up to three touches per turn, including a pass, set, and attack, but no player can hit the ball twice in a row unless blocking. The ball must stay in play, go over the net within the boundary lines, and not touch the ground inside their court. Players rotate clockwise after winning a serve, and back-row players cannot attack from in front of the 3m line. Serves must be made from behind the baseline, and while the ball can touch the net, it must land in play; if it goes out of bounds, the opposing team earns a point. Fouls and violations include net touches, double hits, carrying/lifting the ball, and foot faults if the server steps on or over the baseline before serving. Following these rules ensures fair and competitive gameplay
